Translating Kinetics to Systems of Equations

The model contains two systems of Ordinary Differential equations dependent on two main time-dependent state variables:

  • \( A(t) \): Nab concentrations representing the functional antibodies the body produces in response to the mRNA viruses (IU/ml).
  • \( V(t) \): A mathematical proxy representing the population of transfected host cells expressing the recombinant spike protein.

Model Assumptions

  • \( V(t) \) is a temporary trigger that does not replicate.
  • The body removes transfected cells using the Michaelis-Menten rate.
  • \( A(t) \) multiplies to its carry capacity and then declines over time.
  • Sex does not affect the equations.
  • At the time of the second dose, it is added to any existing leftover dose.

Equation 1: Antibody Concentration (\( A \))

$$ \frac{dA}{dt} = r_1V + r_2AV + A(r_3 - r_4A) $$

Rate In: \( r_1V + r_2AV + r_3A \) (Making new antibodies from the initial trigger, the booster effect, and natural growth).
Rate Out: \( r_4A^2 \) (Antibodies naturally dying off as they become crowded in the blood).

Equation 2: Transfected Cells (\( V \))

$$ \frac{dV}{dt} = \alpha u(t) - \frac{k_1V}{k_2+V} $$

Rate In: \( \alpha u(t) \) (The dose of vaccine cells added directly into the patients’ bodies).
Rate Out: \( \frac{k_1V}{k_2+V} \) (The body's immune system clearing the vaccine cells over time).
